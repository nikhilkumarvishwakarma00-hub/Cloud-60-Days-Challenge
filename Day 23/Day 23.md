# Day 23 - Customer & MVP Blueprint

Restaurant Business

## Objective

Create a Customer & MVP Blueprint for a restaurant startup using AI-powered business planning frameworks.

1. High food prices
2. Slow service
3. Hygiene concerns
4. Limited seating
5. Inconsistent taste
6. Poor online ordering experience
7. Lack of healthy options
8. Parking issues
9. Long waiting times
10. Poor customer support

## Customer Journey

### Awareness
Discovers restaurant through social media, Google Maps, or referrals.

### Consideration
Checks reviews, menu, pricing, and ratings.

### Purchase
Visits restaurant or places an online order.

### Experience
Evaluates food quality, service, and ambiance.

### Retention
Returns due to good experience and value.

## Buying Triggers

- Affordable pricing
- Positive reviews
- Unique menu items
- Fast delivery
- Family-friendly environment

## Customer Objections

- High prices
- Hygiene concerns
- Delivery delays
- Poor reviews

## MVP Recommendation

### Must Have
- Quality food
- Clean environment
- Basic menu
- Fast service

### Should Have
- Online ordering
- Loyalty program
- Home delivery

### Could Have
- Mobile app
- Table reservation system

### Won't Have (MVP)
- Multiple branches
- Premium memberships

## Pricing Hypothesis

- Budget-friendly meals
- Combo offers
- Student discounts

## Top Risks

1. Competition
2. Rising food costs
3. Customer retention
4. Operational challenges
5. Location dependency

## Founder Action Sheet

### Next 30 Days
- Interview 20 potential customers
- Analyze competitors
- Finalize menu
- Test pricing strategy
- Create MVP restaurant experience

## Key Learnings

- Customer research is essential.
- MVP should focus on food quality and service.
- Pricing and customer experience drive retention.
- Validation reduces business risk.

## Outcome

Successfully created a Customer & MVP Blueprint for a restaurant startup and identified the core features needed for launch.
